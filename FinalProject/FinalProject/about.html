Self-Care Tips

  • Take Breaks
    The longer you work on something; the more likely you are to become accustomed to it. As a result, taking brief breaks can be helpful to your overall productivity. Breaks don’t just mean not doing anything, but it can mean doing other relaxing activities as well, like taking a walk or cooking. Breaks can also be doing another task, like washing the dishes.
  • Diversify Self-Care Habits
    If you become too reliant on one activity as self-care, then it may be difficult to relax when you can’t do that activity. For example, if your only self-care activity is art, then in instances where you can’t easily draw, you may feel more stressed because of it. By having a diversity of self-care habits, you ensure you have a variety of ways to destress and care for yourself.
    • Go Outside
      Taking a breath of fresh air, going on a walk, or just even seeing nature can have a positive effect on clearing your mind. If you’re incredibly focused on a certain project or stressed out, then going outside—even briefly—can help you reorient yourself.
    • Take Care of Your Physical Needs
      Along with other basic physical needs (eating, sleeping, etc.), you should make sure you’ve fulfilled each one. This includes social needs as well, such has speaking to a friend or romantic partner. It’s easy to forget to do simple tasks, like drinking water, so you can even set an alarm to remember those.
    • Seek Help, if Necessary
      Remember, you’re never alone. There’s always someone, loved ones and mental health professionals, who are willing to help you. If you feel like you can't face a stressor alone, then you don't have to. Reach out to someone you trust!
